Theme 1 of 10 · Highest frequency

Vascular & embryologic gut origin

~6 questions per exam
Q1 · MCQ
A 52-year-old has stenosis of the artery supplying the cecum. Which artery is involved?
Cecum is midgut → SMA → ileocolic a. (which gives the appendicular branch). Right colic supplies ascending colon, middle colic supplies transverse, left colic + sigmoid + superior rectal are all IMA (hindgut).
Q2 · MCQ
A patient has SMA stenosis distal to the right colic branch. Which structure is most at risk for necrosis?
Distal ileum is supplied by ileal/ileocolic branches, which arise from SMA DOWNSTREAM of the right colic branch. The proximal jejunum + ascending colon branch off SMA upstream and are spared. Sigmoid + descending are IMA (hindgut), unaffected.
Q3 · MCQ
After esophagectomy + partial gastrectomy, the new gastric conduit must remain perfused. Injury to which artery is most catastrophic?
After proximal stomach removal, the L gastric, L gastro-omental, and short gastrics are all sacrificed. The right gastro-omental (off gastroduodenal → common hepatic) is the SOLE remaining supply to the conduit.
Q4 · MCQ
McBurney point appendicitis — the surgeon must ligate a direct branch of which artery?
The full pathway: appendix → appendicular a. → cecal branch of ileocolic → ileocolic → SMA. The surgeon ligates the appendicular's parent vessel = ileocolic.
Q5 · MCQ
Where is the most vulnerable colonic watershed area?
The splenic flexure sits at the SMA/IMA boundary, where the marginal artery of Drummond is often weakest — first to die from hypoperfusion (ischemic colitis).
Fill-in · Embryologic gut origins → vasculature
  1. Foregut = supplied by celiac trunk (T12), drained by portal/splenic vein.
  2. Midgut = supplied by SMA (L1), drained by SMV.
  3. Hindgut = supplied by IMA (L3), drained by IMV → splenic vein → portal vein.
  4. The marginal artery of Drummond connects SMA ↔ IMA.
Quick Recall
List the celiac trunk's 3 main branches.
(1) Common hepatic, (2) Splenic, (3) Left gastric.
Quick Recall
List all 5 branches of the SMA, proximal to distal.
(1) Inferior pancreaticoduodenal, (2) Jejunal & ileal aa., (3) Middle colic, (4) Right colic, (5) Ileocolic (→ appendicular).

Q6 · MCQ
A 22-year-old with new-onset varices in the lower esophagus. The portal-caval anastomosis at this site connects which veins?
Esophageal varices form at the L gastric (portal) ↔ esophageal (azygos/caval) anastomosis. Hemorrhoids = rectal anastomosis (A). Caput medusae = paraumbilical (B).
Theme 2 of 10

Peritoneum

~2-3 questions per exam
Q7 · MCQ
Which structure is intraperitoneal?
The tail of pancreas sits in the splenorenal ligament (intraperitoneal). Head/body/uncinate are retroperitoneal. SAD PUCKER includes ascending/descending colon and kidneys (retroperitoneal). Only 1st part of duodenum is intraperitoneal — 2nd, 3rd, 4th are retroperitoneal.
Q8 · MCQ
A surgeon is performing the Pringle maneuver to control hepatic bleeding. The surgeon's fingers compress the hepatoduodenal ligament. Which structures does this occlude?
The hepatoduodenal ligament is the free edge of the lesser omentum and contains the portal triad: bile duct, hepatic artery proper, portal vein. It also forms the anterior border of the omental foramen.
Q9 · MCQ
Splenectomy via the gastrosplenic ligament puts which gastric region at risk for ischemia?
The gastrosplenic ligament contains the short gastric arteries, which uniquely supply the fundus. Cutting them risks fundus necrosis.
Q10 · MCQ
A patient with a perforated duodenal ulcer is supine when imaged. Where would peritoneal fluid most likely collect first?
Morison's pouch (between liver + R kidney) is the most dependent peritoneal space when SUPINE. When standing it's the rectovesical/rectouterine pouch.
Fill-in · SAD PUCKER (retroperitoneal)
  1. Suprarenals
  2. Aorta & IVC
  3. Duodenum (parts 2-4)
  4. Pancreas (except tail)
  5. Ureters
  6. Colon (asc + desc)
  7. Kidneys
  8. Esophagus (lower)
  9. Rectum (lower 2/3)

Name the 4 boundaries of the omental (epiploic) foramen of Winslow.

Theme 3 of 10

Liver & biliary

~2 questions per exam
Q11 · MCQ
A 45-year-old with ascites + jaundice. CT confirms Budd-Chiari syndrome. Imaging also shows hypertrophy of which liver lobe?
Caudate lobe drains DIRECTLY into IVC (bypasses hepatic veins). When hepatic veins are occluded (Budd-Chiari), only the caudate keeps draining → it hypertrophies. Classic exam Q.
Q12 · MCQ
A 33-year-old with postprandial pain + steatorrhea. ERCP shows obstruction of the duct that empties into the 2nd part of the duodenum. The stone is most likely lodged at:
The ampulla of Vater is the final common pathway for both bile + pancreatic juice → 2nd duodenum. Obstruction here causes both obstructive jaundice + pancreatic enzyme block (steatorrhea).
Q13 · MCQ
During cholecystectomy, the surgeon ligates the cystic artery in Calot's triangle. The cystic artery branches from:
Cystic a. branches from the right hepatic a. inside Calot's triangle (boundaries: cystic duct + common hepatic duct + inferior border of liver).
Quick Recall
Name the 4 lobes of the liver.
Right, Left, Caudate (between IVC + ligamentum venosum), Quadrate (between gallbladder + ligamentum teres).

Fill-in · Liver structures
  1. The caudate lobe sits between the IVC (right) and the ligamentum venosum (left).
  2. The quadrate lobe sits between the gallbladder (right) and the ligamentum teres (left).
  3. The cystic artery is found in Calot's triangle, branching from the right hepatic artery.
  4. The ligamentum venosum is the obliterated ductus venosus; the ligamentum teres is the obliterated umbilical vein.
Theme 4 of 10

Autonomic & referred pain

~2-3 questions per exam
Q14 · MCQ
A 60-year-old with MS has demyelination of the pelvic splanchnic nerves. Parasympathetic input is lost to which structure?
Pelvic splanchnics (S2-S4) supply parasympathetic to HINDGUT only (descending, sigmoid, rectum). Cecum, ascending, transverse colon = MIDGUT, supplied by vagus → unaffected.
Q15 · MCQ
A 32-year-old with sigmoid diverticulitis. Visceral pain refers to which body region?
Hindgut (sigmoid) visceral pain refers to L1-L2 dermatomes = suprapubic. Foregut → epigastric (T5-T9). Midgut → periumbilical (T10-T11). GB/diaphragm → R shoulder (phrenic, C3-C5).
Q16 · MCQ
A patient with foregut peptic ulcer pain. The early visceral pain travels via which sympathetic preganglionic nerve?
Foregut sympathetics: T5-T9 lateral horn → greater splanchnic → celiac ganglion. Pain afferents follow sympathetics back → referred to T5-T9 dermatomes (epigastric).
Q17 · MCQ
A patient with gallbladder inflammation has pain referred to the right shoulder. Which nerve carries this referred pain?
Gallbladder irritation can affect the diaphragm (innervated by phrenic, C3-C5). Referred pain follows the phrenic to the C3-C5 dermatome = shoulder cap (Kehr-like sign).

Quick Recall
Why does the suprarenal medulla NOT need a postganglionic synapse?
The chromaffin cells of the suprarenal medulla ARE the postganglionic sympathetic neurons. They're innervated DIRECTLY by preganglionic greater splanchnic fibers (no synapse in celiac ganglion). They release epi/norepi straight into the bloodstream.
Theme 5 of 10

Posterior abdominal wall & lumbar plexus

~2 questions per exam
Q18 · MCQ
Which lumbar plexus nerve runs on the ANTERIOR surface of the psoas major muscle?
Genitofemoral (L1, L2) is the ONLY lumbar plexus nerve on the anterior surface of psoas. Femoral, LFC, IH, II = lateral. Obturator = medial.
Q19 · MCQ
A 22-year-old hurdler gets a psoas block at L4/L5. After, he has loss of sensation on the medial thigh + weak adduction. Which nerve was injured?
Obturator (L2-L4) emerges MEDIAL to psoas → motor + sensory to medial thigh (adductors). Femoral is lateral → anterior thigh + knee extension.
Q20 · MCQ
A 65-year-old with obesity and a tight belt complains of burning paresthesia over the lateral thigh ("meralgia paresthetica"). Which nerve is compressed?
LFC (L2-L3) crosses the iliacus muscle deep to the inguinal ligament near ASIS. Compression there = meralgia paresthetica. Pure sensory to lateral thigh.
Quick Recall
Name the 6 lumbar plexus nerves (excluding subcostal T12).
(1) Iliohypogastric (L1), (2) Ilioinguinal (L1), (3) Genitofemoral (L1, L2), (4) Lateral femoral cutaneous (L2, L3), (5) Femoral (L2-L4), (6) Obturator (L2-L4).

Theme 6 of 10

Renal & suprarenal

~2 questions per exam
Q21 · MCQ
Hormones from the RIGHT suprarenal gland enter circulation via which vein?
CLASSIC TRAP: right suprarenal v. → IVC directly (NOT through right renal vein). LEFT suprarenal v. → left renal vein → IVC.
Q22 · MCQ
A 28-year-old male presents with left-sided varicocele. The most likely venous anatomy contributing to this is:
Left gonadal v. enters the longer left renal v. at a right angle. Plus possible nutcracker syndrome (left renal v. compressed between SMA and aorta) → backflow → varicocele. Right gonadal goes straight into IVC, less prone.
Q23 · MCQ
A 35-year-old male is diagnosed with testicular cancer. To stage for metastasis, which lymph nodes should be biopsied first?
CLASSIC TRAP: Testis develops at L2 (embryologic origin) → its lymph follows the gonadal vessels back to PARA-AORTIC nodes. Scrotum (skin) → superficial inguinal. Don't pick inguinal — that's the trap!
Q24 · MCQ
A 4-mm kidney stone formed in the renal pelvis becomes mobile. Where does it most likely become lodged FIRST?
3 ureteric constrictions in order: UPJ → pelvic brim → UVJ. The UPJ is FIRST and most likely the first stop for a stone leaving the renal pelvis. UVJ is the most common stuck site for stones already past the brim.
Q25 · MCQ
The inferior suprarenal artery is a branch of:
Each suprarenal gets 3 arteries: superior (← inf. phrenic), middle (← aorta), inferior (← renal a.).
Match · Vein → drainage destination

Right gonadal v.IVC directly

Theme 7 of 10

SMA relationships

~1-2 questions per exam (always)
Q26 · MCQ
A 23-year-old with weight loss has bilious vomiting. CT shows compression of which structure between SMA and aorta?
SMA syndrome: SMA passes anterior to the 3rd part of duodenum, with aorta posterior. Mesenteric fat normally pads the angle; weight loss narrows it → compression → bilious vomiting + early satiety.
Q27 · MCQ
A patient with hematuria + left-sided varicocele. CT shows compression of a structure between SMA and aorta. Which structure?
Nutcracker syndrome: left renal v. crosses anterior to aorta + posterior to SMA → compression. Backs up → hematuria + left varicocele (left gonadal v. drains into left renal v.).
Quick Recall
Name 4 important SMA relationships.
(1) Anterior to 3rd duodenum (SMA syndrome). (2) Anterior to left renal vein (nutcracker syndrome). (3) Posterior to neck of pancreas. (4) Anterior to uncinate process. Originates at L1.
Fill-in · SMA relationships
  1. SMA originates at vertebral level L1.
  2. SMA syndrome compresses the 3rd part of duodenum.
  3. Nutcracker syndrome compresses the left renal vein.
  4. The uncinate process of the pancreas wraps posterior to the SMA.
Theme 8 of 10

Inguinal & spermatic cord

~2 questions per exam
Q28 · MCQ
A 75-year-old man with chronic cough has a hernia bulging MEDIAL to the inferior epigastric artery. What type of hernia?
"MD don't LIe": Medial = Direct. Bulges through Hesselbach's triangle (weak posterior wall, transversalis fascia). Acquired, older men, chronic strain.
Q29 · MCQ
A 28-year-old man has post-appendectomy paresthesia of the anterior scrotum. Which nerve was likely injured?
Ilioinguinal n. (L1) supplies anterior scrotum + medial thigh. Travels through inguinal canal (not deep ring) → most common nerve injured in appendectomy / hernia repair / lower abdominal surgery.
Q30 · MCQ
The cremaster muscle of the spermatic cord is derived from which abdominal wall layer?
Spermatic cord layers (outside-in): External spermatic fascia (← ext. oblique), Cremaster muscle/fascia (← int. oblique), Internal spermatic fascia (← transversalis fascia). Transversus abdominis arches OVER the cord — does NOT contribute a layer.
Q31 · MCQ
A woman has uterine cancer that has spread along the round ligament. Which lymph nodes are sentinel?
The round ligament traverses the inguinal canal and ends in the labia majora. Lymph following it drains to superficial inguinal nodes (uterine fundus has this special drainage route).

Quick Recall
Boundaries of Hesselbach's triangle (mnemonic RIP):
R = Rectus abdominis (medial); I = Inferior epigastric a. (lateral); P = Poupart's (inguinal) ligament (inferior). Direct hernias bulge through here.
Theme 9 of 10

CT cross-sectional anatomy

~1-2 questions per exam
Q32 · MCQ
On an axial CT at the L1 level, you see two large vessels in the midline anterior to the vertebral body. The structure on the patient's RIGHT (your left as the viewer) is:
IVC sits to the patient's right of the aorta. Aorta is left-of-midline, more anterior. IVC is right-of-midline, larger, thinner-walled.
Q33 · MCQ
On axial CT at the pancreas level, the small round vessel just anterior to the aorta (and posterior to the pancreas) is:
SMA branches off aorta at L1 and travels anteriorly behind the pancreas. The classic CT view at this level shows: aorta posterior, SMA emerging anteriorly, SMV right of SMA, left renal v. crossing between aorta and SMA.
Q34 · MCQ
A 55-year-old has an abdominal aortic aneurysm requiring endovascular repair. On CT, the target vessel is identified by which features?
Aorta on CT: left of midline, anterior to vertebral body, thick muscular walls, round. IVC is right-side, larger, oval, thin walls.
Theme 10 of 10

Anterior abdominal wall

~1-2 questions per exam
Q35 · MCQ
A 32-year-old has a stab wound just lateral to the linea alba, in the suprapubic region. The blade enters the rectus muscle. The next layer it perforates is:
Below the arcuate line, there is NO posterior rectus sheath. All 3 aponeuroses pass anterior to rectus. So below arcuate line, only transversalis fascia separates rectus from peritoneum.
Q36 · MCQ
During a TRAM flap reconstruction, the surgeon ligates the inferior epigastric artery. This vessel branches from:
Inferior epigastric is a branch of EXTERNAL iliac (just before it becomes the femoral). Superior epigastric = continuation of internal thoracic.
Q37 · MCQ
The arcuate line is located approximately:
Arcuate line ~midway between umbilicus + pubic symphysis. Below it the posterior rectus sheath disappears. Inferior epigastric a. enters the rectus sheath here.
Fill-in · Anterior wall layers (superficial to deep)
  1. Skin
  2. Camper's fascia (fatty)
  3. Scarpa's fascia (membranous)
  4. External oblique → aponeurosis
  5. Internal oblique → aponeurosis
  6. Transversus abdominis → aponeurosis
  7. Transversalis fascia
  8. Extraperitoneal fat
  9. Parietal peritoneum
Final integration drill

Mixed exam-style MCQs (themes 1-10 combined)

Q38 · Mixed MCQ
A 60-year-old has progressive weight loss + ascites. CT confirms hepatic vein thrombosis (Budd-Chiari). Which finding would be expected on imaging, AND why?
The caudate lobe has its own small veins draining DIRECTLY into IVC, NOT via hepatic veins. So when hepatic veins are obstructed (Budd-Chiari), only the caudate keeps draining → it hypertrophies.
Q39 · Mixed MCQ
A 25-year-old male has periumbilical pain that migrates to McBurney point over 6 hours. The pain is initially periumbilical because:
Appendix = midgut → visceral pain refers to T10 dermatome (umbilicus). When inflammation reaches the parietal peritoneum, somatic afferents take over → sharp, well-localized pain at McBurney point.
Q40 · Mixed MCQ
A 67-year-old male undergoes esophagogastrectomy for gastric cancer. The proximal stomach is removed; a tubular gastric conduit remains. To preserve the conduit's perfusion, the surgeon must NOT damage:
After removing the proximal stomach, L gastric, L gastro-omental, and short gastrics are sacrificed (they all came from the resected segment). The right gastro-omental (← gastroduodenal ← common hepatic) is now the SOLE perfusion source for the conduit.
Q41 · Mixed MCQ
A 42-year-old female with sudden severe RUQ pain radiating to the right shoulder. Murphy sign is positive. The shoulder pain is referred via:
Acute cholecystitis can irritate the diaphragm, which is innervated by phrenic n. (C3-C5). Pain referred to C3-C5 dermatome = shoulder cap. This is the classic Kehr-like sign for GB pathology.
Q42 · Mixed MCQ
A 22-year-old has a stab wound entering the abdominal wall just superior to the inguinal ligament, lateral to the inferior epigastric artery. The structure that traverses this region (and could be injured) is:
The deep inguinal ring is in the transversalis fascia, lateral to the inferior epigastric a. Spermatic cord exits the abdomen through the deep ring at this site (and the round ligament in females).
Q43 · Mixed MCQ
A 55-year-old has bleeding hemorrhoids. The internal hemorrhoids (above the pectinate line) drain via:
Above pectinate line = portal drainage (sup. rectal v. → IMV). Below pectinate line = caval drainage (middle/inf. rectal vv. → internal iliac). Hemorrhoids form at this anastomosis in portal hypertension.
Q44 · Mixed MCQ
During a left adrenalectomy, the surgeon must control which vein, recognizing it drains differently from the right:
Left suprarenal v. → left renal v. → IVC. Right suprarenal v. → IVC directly. Same asymmetry exists for gonadal vv.
Q45 · Mixed MCQ
A 35-year-old male is told he has testicular cancer. CT shows enlarged lymph nodes. Which nodes would you expect to be affected?
CLASSIC TRAP. Testis develops at L2; its lymph follows gonadal vessels to PARA-AORTIC nodes — does NOT drain to inguinal. Inguinal drainage = scrotal SKIN only.
Q46 · Mixed MCQ
A patient has confirmed sigmoid diverticulitis. The visceral afferent pain travels via which nerves to which spinal levels?
Hindgut visceral pain follows sympathetics back to L1-L2 (lumbar splanchnics) → referred to L1-L2 dermatomes (suprapubic). NOT pelvic splanchnics — those carry parasympathetic motor, not pain.
Q47 · Mixed MCQ
A patient has ureteric colic from a stone in the right ureter that just passed the UPJ. Where is the most likely NEXT site for the stone to lodge?
After UPJ, next constriction is the pelvic brim (where ureter crosses anterior to common iliac vessels). Then UVJ (most common stuck site overall).
Q48 · Mixed MCQ
An indirect inguinal hernia in a 7-year-old boy. The herniating bowel descends INTO the scrotum. Through which structure does it pass to enter the scrotum?
Indirect hernia is congenital — patent processus vaginalis. The hernia sac is covered by all 3 spermatic fascial layers (because it travels with the cord through the deep ring). Direct hernia bulges through Hesselbach's triangle (acquired) and only has external spermatic fascia covering.
Q49 · Mixed MCQ
A 45-year-old has chronic pancreatitis. CT shows splenic vein thrombosis. The most likely complication is:
Splenic vein receives short gastric vv. (drain fundus). Splenic v. thrombosis backs up flow → fundus varices specifically. (Sinistral / left-sided portal hypertension — treat with splenectomy.)
Q50 · Mixed MCQ
A 33-year-old female receives a penetrating injury at the right hypochondrium, between the costal margin and umbilicus, midclavicular line. Which organ is most likely injured?
Right hypochondriac region = liver (right lobe) + gallbladder. Spleen = left hypochondriac. Sigmoid = left iliac.
